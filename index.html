My Love for Sweets

If the saying “you are what you eat” is true, I am a very large helping of chocolate, cookies, cake and brownies. I am different from most other girls in one way. I love to eat. My love for food has been apparent since day one when I was a baby and consuming such a large amount. As a toddler I was always hungry and constantly eating. This didn’t seem rare because many toddlers are very active and work up an appetite. I was a competitive gymnast almost my entire life. I believe this drove my metabolism to be double the speed of other girls my age and size. College has really fostered my love for eating and eating often. Unlimited dining halls are like a playground for my hungry tummy.

At home, I eat less often so I have larger meals when I do eat. With the convenience of food being ready all day everyday in each of UGA’s dining halls, I eat often and have smaller meals. My favorite part in each hall is the variety of desserts. I love my sweets. Every dining area has something different to offer. At Bolton you have your traditional cookies and ice creams, with occasional peach cobbler. Ecv has amazing smoothies, strawberry being my favorite, and soft serve ice cream. Ohouse always has fresh made cake. And last but not least, Snelling, who has 24 hours of sweets. These vary from donuts, cookies, and even chocolate bars. There are many times I will be on my way back from class and stop for a Bolton cookie. A day does not pass that I do not have dessert here at UGA. With Bolton and Ohouse being so conveniently located to my dorm, Creswell hall, its quite convenient to go whenever.

We are very lucky to have such a large variety and delicious selection and preparation of the very desserts in these dinning halls. Dessert to me is more than just a piece of cake. I genuinely enjoy food and eating. My friends and family know that I must have dessert at every meal before I consider to be done. Dessert has the amazing power to comfort people and bring them together in times of hardship and stress. Nothing compares to the smile on my face after a nice warm cookie. I have always said “eat your dessert first, for who knows what may happen.” I truly believe in this as dessert has become a huge part of who I am.
